Essay · On Reading in the Age of AI

The Books That Read You Back

Artificial intelligence will hand everyone the same summaries. What it can never hand you is the reader you become.

Oluwafemi Ibukun Williams Petroleum Analyst · DevOps Learner · Writer Lomé · June 2026

In the age of AI, everyone is going to have access to the same summaries. Everyone is going to ask for the same key takeaways. Everyone will generate the same polished notes. The edge is no longer your access to intelligence.

The real edge is what you bring to it as a human being — your judgment, your taste, and a point of view that is uniquely yours.

But there is a much deeper benefit in reading. Because after a while, the books you read start reading you. And by that I mean: they reveal the stories you tell yourself, the fears you protect, the assumptions you have inherited, and the parts of life you still do not understand.

A great book works like a great song. You hear it once and you enjoy it. Then you hear it again years later, and somehow it knows exactly where you are in your life.

The song didn't change.
You did.

That is why I am yet to meet a great leader who does not read.
